Lasagna

Description

Indulge in the rich and comforting flavors of authentic Italian lasagna, layered with a hearty meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and melted mozzarella and Parmesan cheese. Each bite offers a perfect balance of tender pasta, savory meats, and velvety sauce, making it a classic dish that’s perfect for family gatherings or special occasions. This lasagna recipe brings the warmth of traditional Italian cooking to your table, creating a meal that’s both satisfying and unforgettable.

Serves: 6-8

Ingredients

For the Meat Sauce:

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 celery stalk
  • 2 cans of crushed tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup of tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup of red wine
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

For the Bechamel Sauce:

  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups whole milk, warmed
  • Salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg to taste

For Assembly:

  • 12 lasagana noodles (or fresh lasagna sheets)
  • 2 cups grated mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions

    Prepare the meat sauce:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, garlic, carrot, and celery. Cook until the vegetables are soft and the onion is translucent.
  2. Add the ground beef and pork. Cook until browned, breaking the meat apart with a spoon
  3. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for another 2 minutes
  4. Pour in the red wine and cook until the wine has mostly evaporated.
  5. Add the crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Stir well, reduce the heat, and let it simmer for at least 30 minutes, stirring occasionally. The sauce should thicken and become rich in flavor.

    6. Prepare the bechamel sauce:

  6. In a separate saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour and whisk continuously to create a roux. Cook for 1-2 minutes without browning the flour.
  7. Gradually add the warmed milk while whisking to avoid lumps. Continue whisking until the sauce thickens and becomes smooth.
  8. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg. Remove from heat.

    9. Cook the lasagna noodles:

  9. If using dried lasagna noodles, cook them in salted boiling water according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside. If using fresh lasagna sheets, you can skip this step.

    10. Assemble the lasagna:

  10. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  11. In a large baking dish, spread a thin layer of the meat sauce on the bottom.
  12. Place a layer of lasagna noodles on top of the sauce.
  13. Spread a layer of the meat sauce over the noodles, followed by a layer of béchamel sauce.
  14. Sprinkle a handful of grated mozzarella and Parmesan cheese over the sauces.
  15. Repeat the layers until all the ingredients are used, ending with a layer of béchamel sauce and a generous topping of mozzarella and Parmesan.

    16. Bake the lasgana:

  16. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes.
  17. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbling.

    18. Rest and serve:

  18. Allow the lasagna to rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing.
  19. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve.
